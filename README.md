# Healthcare-Claims-Data-Analysis- PLEASE HAVE A LOOK AT THE PPT ATTACHED
My primary objective was to present complex healthcare analytics in a way that is intuitive and easily understandable for non-technical audiences.
A healthcare data analytics project focused on analyzing Medicare beneficiary chronic conditions and outpatient claims data to uncover healthcare cost drivers, provider spending patterns, and chronic illness trends using Python.

## Project Overview

Using Medicare Beneficiary Summary and Outpatient Claims datasets, the analysis connected chronic condition indicators with real healthcare expenditure data to identify:

- High-cost chronic illness groups
- Cost-per-member trends
- Provider-level spending variations
- High-risk patient populations
- Opportunities for cost optimization

The project combined healthcare analytics, statistical benchmarking, and data visualization to generate actionable business and operational insights.

## Analytical Approach

The analysis linked beneficiary chronic conditions with outpatient claim costs to evaluate healthcare spending behavior and provider variation.

Key analytical methods included:

- Cost-per-member analysis
- Chronic condition segmentation
- Provider benchmarking
- Z-score standardization
- Outlier detection
- Data visualization and trend analysis

---

## Key Insights

### Chronic Illness Burden
Patients with multiple chronic conditions represented the largest clinical burden and generated the highest outpatient costs.

### Cost Drivers
Condition combinations involving:
- COPD
- Cancer
- Diabetes
- Stroke-related illnesses

showed significantly higher per-member healthcare costs.

### Provider Benchmarking
Provider spending patterns were standardized using z-scores to identify:
- Efficient providers
- Consistently high-cost providers
- Chronic-condition-specific outliers

### Operational Findings
The “Multiple Conditions” patient group emerged as both:
- the most common, and
- the most expensive,

making it a critical focus area for healthcare cost management.
